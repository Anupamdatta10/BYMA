BYMA(Baruipur young men's association)

About the organization

BARUIPUR YOUNG MENSâ€™ ASSOCIATION (BYMA) a registered, non-political, non-racial voluntary social welfare organization was established on 1940 with the basic objective of empowering the poor people to be united and demand for their legal rights. Initially, BYMA was an informal organization. In the year 1988, it was registered under West Bengal Societies Registration Act, XXVI of 1961. We not only work for the rural people but we also started work with the poor people respecting their decisions and giving much value on their plans and action. BYMA aspires to improve the living conditions of the poor and marginalized by adopting strategies through community initiative, participation and sensitization. We have worked among backward communities in the interior unreached pockets of West Bengal in India.

OUR OBJECTIVES

The principal objective of our Association is to reach people a point from where they can function on their own without further help from others.
  • To provide quality education for the downtrodden people.
  • To provide free health services including promotion of health related activities and also to conduct health awareness programme through simple yogic practices.
  • To provide relief works for the people affected by natural calamities in distress.
  • To provide training for womenfolk for their economic upliftment as well as to improve the low status of women.
  • To spread awareness among people for the necessity of clean environment and to undertake projects such as cleaning of water bodies, water conservation practices, plantations, healthy sanitary practices, judicious use of plastics etc.
  • To undertake rural development projects that will ensure upliftment of standard of living in villages.
  • To promote sympathy, tolerance, good feeling and unity among the people of different faiths, so as to evolve a high sense of social goodwill and peaceful co-existence.
